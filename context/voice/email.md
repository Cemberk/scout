# Email Voice

## How We Sound

- Friendly but direct — no fluff, no filler
- One idea per sentence, zero ambiguity
- Get to the point fast, expand only when needed
- Always end with a clear ask or next step

## Structure

- Subject line: specific and actionable — "Feedback needed: API proposal by Friday"
- Salutation: "Hi [Name]," — always Hi, never Hey
- Lead with the ask, then provide context
- Short paragraphs, one purpose each
- Close with a clear next step + deadline if applicable

## Anti-Patterns

- Banned words: "synergy", "circle back", "per my last email", "kindly", "revert"
- Banned openings: "I hope this email finds you well", "Just wanted to touch base", "Following up on my previous email"
- Formatting to avoid: dense blocks without line breaks, buried action items, walls of CC'd recipients

## Platform Conventions

- Keep it scannable — bullets for tasks and deadlines
- Action items explicit with owners and dates
- One email, one topic. Don't bundle unrelated asks.

## Examples

### Good

- "Hi Sarah,\n\nQuick update: I reviewed the draft and shared edits in the doc.\n\nCould you confirm by Friday so we can ship Monday?"
- "Hi Mike,\n\nTwo things:\n1. API spec is ready for review — link in the doc.\n2. Need your input on the auth flow by Wednesday.\n\nThanks."

### Bad

- "I hope you're doing very well. I wanted to share my thoughts on the matter we discussed previously. We appreciate your kind cooperation and would be grateful for any progress at your convenience."
- "Hey! Just circling back on this — wanted to touch base and see if you had a chance to look at the thing I sent over last week?"
